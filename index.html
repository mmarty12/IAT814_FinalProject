About the dataset
This visualization is based on a large dataset of approximately 90,000 board games, originally sourced from BoardGameGeek and found on Kaggle.
The dataset includes key attributes such as user ratings, number of votes, genres, mechanics, playtime, and release year, enabling exploration of popularity patterns and design trends across games.
Thess visualizations are primarily designed for casual and family players who want to discover popular and accessible games. It can also support quick trend exploration for reviewers or designers, though it is not intended as a deep analytical tool.

Mechanics × Categories × Ratings
How to read this section
1
Heatmap shows Mechanic × Category relation. Each cell shows the average community rating for games that combine a specific mechanic (rows) with a specific category (columns). Darker green means a higher average rating.

Note: the scale runs from the lowest to the highest value found in the data, so colours are relative to this chart only.

2
A dash (—) means no data. That mechanic and category combination had fewer than 5 games in the dataset, so no reliable average could be computed.
3
Dot plot represents Top combinations. The chart below ranks the eight highest-rated mechanic–category pairs. The horizontal axis is scaled tightly around the actual value range, so small differences between dots are meaningful.
HINT: What to look for? Combinations that score consistently above 7.0 tend to be niche, highly-engaged audiences. A mechanic that scores high across multiple categories (entire row is dark) is a strong design signal — players reward it regardless of theme.
